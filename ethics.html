IT-Related Ethics

Ethics in technology means making decisions that protect people, reduce harm, and support fairness, privacy, security, and accountability.

Why IT Ethics Matters

Technology affects how people communicate, learn, work, shop, and access services. Because of this, IT professionals should think carefully about the impact of the systems they build. Ethical technology should respect users, protect sensitive information, and avoid creating unnecessary harm.

Guidelines for Developers

  • Build software that supports the public good and avoids harm.
  • Protect user privacy and collect only the data that is necessary.
  • Design secure systems that reduce the risk of data leaks or misuse.
  • Be honest about what software can and cannot do.
  • Test products carefully before releasing them to users.

Ethical Considerations for Data Scientists

  • Use accurate and appropriate data for analysis.
  • Check for bias in datasets and algorithms.
  • Explain results clearly so people do not misunderstand the data.
  • Protect confidential or personal information.
  • Consider how data-based decisions may affect different groups of people.

Professional Responsibility

The ACM Code of Ethics explains that computing professionals should contribute to society and human well-being, avoid harm, be honest, respect privacy, and give proper credit for intellectual work. The IEEE/ACM Software Engineering Code of Ethics also emphasizes that software engineers should act consistently with the public interest and maintain high professional standards.
